Thrust Boring

Thrust boring is a method of making a horizontal bored hole. It is commonly implemented under the road, shallow river, or railways from launch shaft to target shaft. Our boring method uses the combination of water flush and thrust boring which makes it be able to work in almost any kind of soil.
First, the work holes and target holes need to be prepared. Drilling starts by using a pilot pipe to reach the target hole. Drilled soil pieces are removed from the hole by the flow of water from the drill handle. To increase the diameter of the hole, the reamer bit is pulled or pressed by following the pilot pipe that has been installed in the right path. Casing or pipe is installed in the hole after the hole has been cleaned from dirt.

Capabilities

  • Water & gas service lines & conduit installation
  • Bore up to 120 m
  • Bore diameter ranged from 98mm to 800mm
  • Thrust capacity of 16 tons and pullback 8 tons
  • Boring method combination of water flush, continuous auger, and thrust boring
  • Can be implemented for almost any kind of soil

Work Hole

The work hole is designed to be able to withstand the force of the equipment jack as well as the weight of the equipment. The size of the work hole generally has a length of 6m x 2m width with 0.60m depth below the center of drilling, which depends on the type of tool. Safe entry points and workspaces for our workers in work holes must also be provided while making work holes. The design of the work hole must also cover the stability of the holder due to the force of the jack. The design of the work hole is adapted to the type of soil and the jack load.
The water pit (sump pit) must be at the bottom of the work hole so that the pump can be installed. The size of the pit holes must be adjusted to the depth of the hole and the volume of water for the purpose of drilling.

Target Hole

The target hole is the final end of the drilling. This hole can be accessed to the issuance of guide pipe, casing, or pipe / cable entry. The target hole width can be adjusted according to the hole width of the Open Cut method.

Working Method

The guide pipe is used to do thrust boring work.

Step 1: 1. Pilot Hole

The pilot pipe is jacked and rotated through the soil until it reaches the target shaft. At the end of pilot pipe is equipped with cutting bit to cut the soil. Debris is transported by water that flushes from drilling rod and return through the gaps between the pilot pipes and the drilling rods.

Step 2: Reaming

Drilling process is guided by pilot pipe lay precisely on target line. The soil is cut by bits and debris is transported by water flush. This work can be started from launch pit by push-in method or from target pit by pull-in method. This process continues until the drilling bit reaches the target shaft.

Step 3: Cleaning

After the desired hole is achieved and the reamer has passed through it completely, a mud pass or packer reamer is implemented to assure that the hole is clean from all excavated material and that the drill fluid has filled the hole completely. It is for allowing smooth lubricated pull back of the pipe, avoiding friction of the pull section.
