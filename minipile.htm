Minipile

HMiniple

Minipile is a small bored pile with diameter ranged from 300 - 600 mm. The hole is drilled using wash boring method and cleaned by water flush until the overflow water is clean. Pile is reinforced by deformed steel bars with stirrups to develop moment capacity and to maintain concrete solidity. The concrete pouring uses tremie (multiple segment pipes up to hole bottom) or grouting pump if required. Pilecap is installed at the top of the pile.

1. SURVEY AND SET UP METHOD

The location can be implemented in the middle and/or on the edge of an existing building. The height of the drilling machine plus the handlebar drill is about 270cm. For the purpose of excavating mud/soil disposal, it is required to create disposal access to the outside of the building. Drilling mud will be collected in a pit outside the building. From the reservoir, the mud / water will be flowed out through the sewers outside and then flowed into the river or pond near the work site.

2. DRILLING PROCESS

Drilling is carried out using the wash boring method with a fish tail bit diameter. The length of the drilling rod is 1.5 m. If required, we will use a casing mounted on the top side of the drilling to avoid sliding. After drilling reaches the desired depth, a hole cleaning process will be carried out by flowing water into the hole until a clean water output is obtained.

3. STEEL INSTALLATION

For the implementation of piles installation in a room that has a low head room, the stirrup ring installation will be carried out when installing the iron into the drilling hole. A hole / opening is needed in the wall to allow the iron to be inserted from outside the room by being directed to the drilling hole. For pile locations outside the room, stirrups will be installed before the process of entering the iron into the dug hole.

4. GROUTING

Grouting is done by using a tremie pipe. Tremie will be installed immediately after mounting the iron in the middle pile position. The tremie pipe consists of several segments which are joined to reach the depth of the dug hole. At the top of the tremie funnel will be installed as a tool to insert concrete.

Through the tremie pipe, grouting is carried out starting from the bottom position of the pile gradually to fill the dug hole. The bottom end of the tremie pipe will always be kept submerged by grouting concrete with a minimum depth of 1m. This grouting process will be carried out until clean concrete is obtained from the drilling hole.
