Bathymetry

Bathymetry survey is a survey method of recording underwater depth of the ocean floor or lake floor. The output of this survey is an underwater contour map of the surveyed area under the water. The purpose of this survey is to obtain information of seafloor or lake condition so it can be used as a data for example: oil rig installation, seafloor condition, fish location, etc.

Bathymetry survey generally uses a sonar unit attached to a survey boat (manned or unmanned). As the boat going and scanning around the targeted area by using the sound waves from the boat. As the waves hit the bottom, they bounce off and are picked up by the sonar unit. From that, we can gain the information of the distance between water surface and the bottom. The sonar unit is equipped with a GPS system so each surveyed area of the map can be specifically recorded with the help of the satellite.
