Drilling

Seismic Shothole

Seismic shothole drilling can be used to allow installation of shotgun or explosive to develop ground vibration shock as a signal source for seismic survey.

Site Clearance and Drainage

Before work can be started, site must be prepared properly; suitable drainage system must be constructed and maintained throughout whole work process.Surface cleaning, levelling, and light compacting may be required to support the drilling and testing works. Surface drain will be provided at shot hole area, allowing water splash from testing to be flowed back into the holes.

Site Arregement

Casing Installation



Night Work Lighting

Since working at night may be required, adequate lighting must be provided. The lamps will be installed to a stand at least 1.2 m above the ground.

Gun Support Frame

The frame is portable lifting equipment which is provided to hang the pressure gun. The load capacity of the frame is 0.5 ton with 1.6m clearance distance from the ground.

Drilling Works

When drill moves forward through the ground (the rotary method), soil is automatically flushed out by the pressure of the drilling fluid through the annular space between the outer surface of the drill stem and the inner surface of the borehole. The rod stabilizer can be used to tie the alignment of the drill stem and will be installed in the middle range of the total drilling.

The progress of drilling is measured by the number of rods penetrated into the ground. The effective length of one section rod is 1.5m. After final depth of drilling reached, a tape measuring device is lowered into the hole to check the depth. In order to minimize the sedimentation, hole cleaning process may be required by using water flow from the rod to allow debris removed. The quality of water overflow from the hole will indicate the cleanness of the water inside the hole. After the overflowed water is clean then the water flow stops and the drilling rod will be pulled up from the hole.

The overflowed water from the hole in the drilling and cleaning process will be collected in the surface settling pit. The clean water at the top of settling pit flows or pumps to water pit, allowing it to be reused for drilling and cleaning operation.

The data obtained in boring process are recorded in the field log (Bore log) and included the following:

1. Project identification

2. Boring number

3. Location

4. Orientation

5. Date boring began

7. Drillers name

8. Soil classifications / descriptions (hardness, colour, degree of weathering, and other pertinent identities)

9. Groundwater condition

10. And disturbed sampling
