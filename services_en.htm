DRILLING SURVEY FOUNDATION SLOPE STABILITY

The company has a mission to provide a wide range of alternative soil application technology. Soil underground has varied condition and has varied potential hazard. It may be required to have pre-construction work such as soil stability, ground water level control, or excavation system. Improvement work may be required such as underpinning, settlement control, or foundation reinforcement.

Thrust Boring

Thrust boring is a method of making a horizontal bored hole. It is commonly implemented under the road, shallow river, or railways from launch shaft to target shaft. Our boring method uses the combination of water flush and thrust boring which makes it be able to work in almost any kind of soil.

Soil Investigation

The objective of the soil investigation services is to investigate geological and geotechnical condition for various purposes such as foundation design, mining exploration, slope stability, road building, etc. Our service covers coring and non-coring drilling and in-situ testing such as Standard Penetration Test (SPT), Cone Penetration Test (CPT), Field Vane Shear Test and Permeability Test. Disturbed and undisturbed samplings taken from the borehole are analysed at our soil laboratory for soil index and engineering properties such as water content, density, particle size grading, Atterberg limits, specific gravity, unconfined compression test, triaxial test, direct shear test, consolidation, etc.

Bathymetry

Bathymetry survey is a survey method of recording underwater depth of the ocean floor or lake floor. The output of this survey is an underwater contour map of the surveyed area under the water. The purpose of this survey is to obtain information of seafloor or lake condition so it can be used as a data for example: oil rig installation, seafloor condition, fish location, etc.

Minipile

Minipile is a small bored pile with diameter ranged from 300 - 600 mm. The hole is drilled using wash boring method and cleaned by water flush until the overflow water is clean. Pile is reinforced by deformed steel bars with stirrups to develop moment capacity and to maintain concrete solidity. The concrete pouring uses tremie (multiple segment pipes up to hole bottom) or grouting pump if required. Pilecap is installed at the top of the pile.

Micropiles

Micropiles are small diameter poles that can be installed in almost any type of soil, with design loads as small as 3 tons and up to 100+ tons. Micropiles are a reliable alternative to conventional pile foundation techniques, especially for areas with limited access or short workspaces.

Soil Nailing

Soil nailing is a soil/slope reinforcement method in site to keep soil stability by adding arelatively small reinforcement (generally steel bar) with space that is close to the ground mass so the soil becomes stable.

Micropile Slide Stabilization

Micropile slide stabilization is used to stabilize the slope in all types of soil and rocks. earth marks are formed by connecting micropile lines, sometimes combined with ground anchors, into concrete beams made on the slope surface. Working as a tensile and compressive rod, the micropile forms an integrally slope stabilization system that is able to withstand the forces causing slide.

Gabion Installation

Gabion is a welded wire cage or box filled with materials such as stone, concrete, sand, or soil that is installed on the slope like a block construction. The purpose of gabion installation is to help improving slope stability, preventing land slide, and giving erosion protection in slope.

Slope Protection

Rockfall protection with using Steelgrid wiremesh is a solution of applying slope protection from falling rocks with active method (with anchors) and passive methods (without anchors on slopes). Protection will be applied on the slope surface so that loose material on the slope surface does not fall and endanger the road users. If necessary, a combination of wiremesh installation and soil nailing systems can be applied if the slope needs additional reinforcement.
